How to Read a Photograph

Photographs are NOT neutral. Someone made choices.

The 5-Step Framework

  1. Observe
  2. Question
  3. Context
  4. Conclude
  5. Message
⚠️ You do NOT need to follow the steps in order. Real analysis moves back and forth.

Step 1 — Observe

  • Who / what can you see?
  • Where are they?
  • What details stand out?

Step 2 — Question

  • What don’t you understand?
  • What might be happening?
  • Who took this and why?

Step 3 — Context

  • Where / when is this?
  • What real situation could this connect to?

Context comes AFTER your ideas — not before.

Step 4 — Make a Decision

  • What do you think is happening? (Choose ONE idea)
  • What evidence supports it?
  • What could be another interpretation?
  • What might be missing?

Step 5 — Message

Write TWO sentences:

1. The photographer wants us to think / feel ______ because ______.

2. However, another possible message is ______ because ______.
